Polpette

Description

A South Italian favorite that you can easily make at home! Neapolitan Eggplant Meatballs (Polpette di Melanzane) are excellent for snacks or appetizers – a vegetable treat that is always a hit!

Ingredients

  • Eggplant – you can use any type of eggplant you have, European or Asian; both will work excellently with this recipe.
  • Parsley – fresh parsley, if possible
  • Salt & pepper – just use your favorite brand.
  • Parmigiano Reggiano – freshly grated, if possible,
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Egg – beaten.
  • Vegetable oil – if you prefer, you can use a bit of olive oil for roasting the eggplant; but it needs to be vegetable oil for frying.

Steps

  1. Start by preheating the oven to 225°C (400°F).
  2. Pour oil on the eggplants and place them on a baking tray (photo 1).
  3. Once the oven is ready, place the baking tray inside for twenty-five to thirty minutes or until the eggplants have softened; then set them aside until they’re cool enough to handle.
  4. Scoop the eggplants’ insides with a spoon and place them on a strainer to drain (photo 2).
  5. Once the eggplants have been drained, place them in a medium bowl and add the parsley, cheese, half a cup of breadcrumbs, salt, pepper, and egg (photo 3).
  6. Mix with a fork to combine the mixture evenly (photo 4).
  7. Use a tablespoon to take a portion of the mixture and form it into a ball.
  8. Roll each ball in the remaining breadcrumbs (photo 5).
  9. Using a medium-sized saucepan, heat vegetable oil.
  10. Once the oil is ready, start cooking the eggplant meatballs (photo 6).
  11. Once the balls turned brown on all sides, take them out of the pan (photo 7).
  12. Place them on top of a kitchen paper to remove excess oil (photo 8).
